# Using the LibCard name

LibCard is **meant to be forked.** Making your own card from this template is the
whole point — and you do **not** have to rename anything to do it. Your fork can
stay called LibCard. 🎉

Here's the simple version.

### ✅ Totally fine — this is the intended use

- Fork the repo, **keep the "LibCard" name**, and build your own card.
- Customize anything — themes, links, content, even the code.
- Use it for yourself, your company, or pages you set up for other people.

### 🙏 Please keep (it's already there by default)

- The **"Made with LibCard"** link in the page footer, pointing back to the
  original project. It costs you nothing, and it's how other people find LibCard.

### 🚫 Not okay — this is what "trademark" is about

- Claiming **you created** LibCard.
- Presenting your fork as the **official** LibCard project, or as endorsed by it.
- Using the **LibCard name or logo to market a separate, competing product**
  (e.g. relaunching it under the LibCard name as your own paid template or SaaS).
- Anything that makes people think your version *is* LibCard.

---

In one line: **use it, keep the name, just don't pass it off as your own
creation.**

The MIT [LICENSE](./LICENSE) covers the *code*. This note covers the *name* — and
it mostly comes down to honest attribution, which the default footer link already
handles for you. Trademark law is about preventing confusion over who made
something, not about stopping you from forking; a personal fork that keeps the
footer link is exactly the kind of use that's welcome.

Questions about using the LibCard name? Open an issue.
